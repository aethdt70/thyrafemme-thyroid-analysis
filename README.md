## ThyraFemme Balance Ingredients: The Full Breakdown

The formula contains 14 active ingredients. Let me walk through the heavy hitters and what they actually do — not the marketing version, but the version backed by what we know from nutritional biochemistry.

### Iodine (from Kelp & Bladderwrack)

Iodine is non-negotiable for thyroid function. Your thyroid gland traps iodine from your bloodstream and uses it to manufacture T3 and T4. Without enough iodine, production slows. Kelp and bladderwrack — both sea vegetables — are among the richest natural sources of iodine, and they deliver it in a form your body absorbs efficiently.

### L-Tyrosine

Think of L-tyrosine as the scaffolding. It's an amino acid that physically bonds with iodine molecules to form thyroid hormones. If you're iodine-sufficient but tyrosine-deficient, you're still running at half capacity. L-tyrosine also supports dopamine and norepinephrine production — which explains why people with low thyroid often report brain fog and low motivation.

### Zinc & Selenium

These two minerals do the behind-the-scenes work. Selenium is required by the enzymes that convert inactive T4 into active T3. Without it, your body can produce all the thyroid hormone it wants — and none of it will do anything useful. Zinc supports the same conversion pathway and also plays a role in immune regulation, which matters because the most common cause of hypothyroidism (Hashimoto's) is autoimmune.

### Vitamin B12 & Magnesium

Low B12 and thyroid dysfunction often travel together. B12 deficiency mimics hypothyroid symptoms almost perfectly: fatigue, weakness, memory problems, mood disturbances. Magnesium supports over 300 enzymatic reactions in the body, including those involved in energy production and hormone regulation. It also helps with sleep — something a lot of women with thyroid issues struggle with.

### Ashwagandha & Schisandra

Ashwagandha is an adaptogen with a decent body of research behind it for reducing cortisol. Elevated cortisol suppresses thyroid function. Schisandra is less studied in Western literature but has a long history in Traditional Chinese Medicine for supporting the endocrine system and liver detoxification — both relevant when your hormones are out of whack.

### Cayenne Pepper, Copper, Manganese, Molybdenum

The supporting cast. Cayenne supports circulation (better blood flow = better nutrient delivery to the thyroid). Copper, manganese, and molybdenum are trace minerals that act as enzyme cofactors in metabolic and antioxidant pathways.
